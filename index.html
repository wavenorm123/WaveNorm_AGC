WaveNorm AGC

Adaptive Gain Control (AGC) is a technique used to keep audio loudness consistent. In real recordings, speech can be too quiet, too loud, or vary over time depending on distance, environment, and background noise. AGC automatically adjusts the volume to make speech easier to hear.

However, not all AGC methods behave the same way. Some may amplify background noise, introduce sudden changes in loudness, or affect the natural quality of speech.

This page compares different AGC approaches, including a proposed method called WaveNorm AGC. The goal is to observe how each method handles:

  • Consistency of speech loudness
  • Behavior during silence or pauses
  • Background noise amplification
  • Smoothness of volume changes
